A little bit about me

My interest in computers and math spans as far back as I can remember. As a child in primary and elementary school I was very interested in programing. I’ve learned Basic, Logo and Visual Basic. I excelled in computer classes and I used to help my friends out with these classes. In high school I had to choose a specialization as my country’s educational curriculum requires choosing between math, science and liberal arts. I chose math as a specialization to be able to qualify for applying to engineering and computer science majors at universities. I believe that my chosen career will enable me to fulfill my ambitions in succeeding as I personally define success in life as strive to do what you mostly enjoy to maximize contribution to human knowledge and reflect on yourself as an educated human being. Throughout my life I have participated in extracurricular activities through various volunteer experiences. I have striven for academic excellence, always wishing to make the most of my education. In addition, I have managed to work to provide some of my basic educational expenses.

I enrolled in the College of Engineering at Ain Shams University one of the highly ranked engineering schools in Egypt. After the first year where I got exposed to a whole lot of variety of general engineering classes, I knew that I wanted to study electrical engineering as a major with a specialization in computer engineering. There I started focusing on software classes and developing my skill and knowledge in this area. As an engineering student who was focusing on computers I got exposed to a lot of courses that involved hardware and control engineering as well as math which gave me a whole new dimension of knowledge in computing in fact I knew that my real passion was software but I wanted to learn everything about computers. The main challenge in my undergraduate was the graduation project where me and my team wanted to address a problem that was developing in our city; traffic specifically we wanted to find a way to reduce the high number of accidents per year that happened at intersections across the city of Cairo. We thought of creating an integrated hardware/software system that involved avoiding collisions at street intersections based on GPS technology and at the same time all vehicles being connected to servers on the streets to allow the traffic police control center to monitor. I took the part of developing all software aspects of the project where I programmed the chip and the GPS that goes on the vehicle and did the server side programming as well.
After graduation I worked as a software developer in different countries in the North African area. I was mainly working in the telecom software field developing and maintaining billing systems and customer relationship management systems through different phases of the software lifecycle. As an employee in a medium profile company I got to wear different hats. I remember that project that I was a part of the development team and I was the main player in the implementation team dealing with customer change requests. This gave me a wide exposure to work with different systems and see all kinds of problems in dealing with databases and web interfaces.
Also during these years I passed the NCEES FE Engineer in Training certification exam which wrapped up all my engineering experiences until that point. At that time I knew that I wanted to continue in academia and learn more through an academic degree. So I decided to come to the USA to earn a Master of Science degree in Information Technology. I enrolled in Arkansas Tech University where it was an exciting place to be. I took a variety of classes in different levels of computing; database, networks, web design and software engineering. I got an assistantship and worked as a graduate assistant; helping professors in grading and teaching labs also working with the international office organizing international events and helping students.
I faced a couple of challenges through my masters years the first was time management as I was working as an assistant and attending classes with all the work associated at the same time, I maintained a GPA of 4.0 across all my classes and an overall graduation GPA of 3.92. The second challenge was my graduation project during the last semester I took on a hardcore software engineering project titled “Visual programming and algorithm animation using Genesis” which was basically a tool that aids newbie programming students to understand the basic concepts of programming through visual algorithm animation using a dynamic grammar programming language that was developed by another research group in the department.
Throughout my experiences in software I mastered some tools that allowed me to use my skills like Java. I’ve been exposed to different layers and stages of the development of information systems starting from the database going through the data access layer, the business layer and the presentation layer I’ve gained experience in these kinds of systems being implemented on different environments. I was always interested in increasing the performance and usability of multi-tier information systems, but mainly database and programming languages are my preferred specialization. I look forward to take teaching as a career, Also my passions for research can root down to my deep need to always learn something new, this passion to learn and teach is my main drive in life.
The truth is I believe in education, research and development. I vowed to myself to keep learning and never to stop as that’s what I enjoy most. Research in its own is a mean not a goal; it is the mean to knowledge and development. I believe that the field of computing has a non-stop growing potential research areas that I will be very interested in, based on my work experience and graduate degree. This program will give me the chance to broaden my knowledge and be able to pass it on as a potential teacher and researcher.
