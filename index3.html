About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Texas Woman's University and a recent Ph.D. graduate from the University of Arkansas at Little Rock, where my work focused on automating data integration and curation for knowledge discovery. Using Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning, I develop methods to identify and extract underlying entities from diverse data sources.

My research vision is to aid and automate scientific discovery by developing ethical AI systems capable of integrating, cleaning, and mining scientific information automatically. My interests span data quality in AI, biomedical data integration using Knowledge Graphs, and graph-based representation learning for applications like search engines and entity matching.
